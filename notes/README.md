# Notes

This directory contains my personal research notes developed throughout my work in experimental physics and related disciplines.

Rather than polished publications, these documents are **living notes** that evolve alongside my research. They combine theoretical derivations, literature summaries, laboratory practices, technical references, and worked examples into a growing personal reference library. Their primary purpose is to organize knowledge, clarify concepts through writing, and provide a reliable resource for future projects and collaborations.

Most notes are written in LaTeX and are intended to bridge the gap between lecture notes, laboratory manuals, and traditional textbooks. While they are primarily written for my own use, I hope they may also prove useful to students and researchers with similar interests.

## Contents

### Chemistry

General chemistry notes covering the language, nomenclature, quantitative methods, molecular structure, and laboratory concepts commonly encountered in modern experimental science.

Planned topics include:

- Chemical language and nomenclature
- Chemical reactions and stoichiometry
- Atomic structure and chemical bonding
- Molecular geometry and crystal symmetry
- Solutions and concentrations

### Spectroscopy

Notes on the theoretical and experimental foundations of optical spectroscopy.

Topics include:

- Raman spectroscopy
- Fourier and angle-resolved spectroscopy
- Angle-resolved spectroscopy
- Pump–probe spectroscopy
- Optical characterization techniques
- Data analysis and calibration

### Optical Elements

A practical reference to the optical components most frequently encountered in spectroscopy and optical laboratories.

Planned topics include:

- Lenses
- Mirrors
- Polarizers
- Beam splitters
- Prisms
- Objectives

Each topic will include concise descriptions, optical diagrams, basic operating principles, practical alignment notes, and common laboratory applications.

## Future topics

As the repository grows, additional notes will cover areas including:

- Strong light–matter interaction
- Quantum optics
- Computational physics
- Scientific programming
- Mathematical methods for physics
